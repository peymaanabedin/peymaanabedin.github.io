Lens & IR Detector

In a dark room, press Start. Slowly scan suspicious items (clocks, smoke detectors). Watch the screen for a bright, star-like glint from a lens, or faint purple/white dots from night vision IR LEDs.

EMF Field Detector

Powered devices emit an electromagnetic field. Move your phone slowly over surfaces. A sharp spike indicates hidden electronics and will trigger a vibration.

Bluetooth Scanner

Some cameras broadcast a Bluetooth signal for setup. Press scan to look for nearby devices. Unfamiliar names could be suspicious.

Guided Network Audit

Hidden Wi-Fi cameras are connected to the local network. Log into your router to see a list of all connected devices. Look for anything you don't recognize.

  • Step 1: Connect to the Wi-Fi network.
  • Step 2: Tap one of these common router IPs to open the admin page:
  • 192.168.1.1
  • 192.168.0.1
  • 192.168.1.254
  • 10.0.0.1
  • Step 3: Log in. (Admin passwords are often on the router itself).
  • Step 4: Find the "Attached Devices," "Client List," or "DHCP List" page.
  • Step 5: Compare the list to your known devices (phones, laptops, TVs). Investigate any unfamiliar device names or MAC addresses.
